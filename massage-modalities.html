Massage Modalities

Swedish Massage
One of the most commonly taught and well-known massage techniques, Swedish massage is a treatment designed to energize the body by stimulating circulation. Five basic strokes, all flowing toward the heart, are used to manipulate the soft tissues of the body. The disrobed client is covered by a sheet, with only the area being worked on exposed. Therapists use a combination of kneading, rolling, vibrational, percussive, and tapping movements, with the application of oil to reduce friction on the skin. The many benefits of Swedish massage may include generalized relaxation, dissolution of scar tissue adhesions, and improved circulation, which may speed healing and reduce swelling from injury.                                                                                                 

Deep Tissue Massage
Deep-tissue massage affects the sub-layer of musculature and fascia. The muscles must be relaxed in order to effectively perform deep-tissue massage, otherwise tight surface muscles prevent the therapist from reaching deeper musculature. It helps with chronic muscular pain and injury rehabilitation and reduces inflammation-related pain caused by arthritis and tendinitis. It is generally integrated with other massage techniques.

Shiatsu Massage
Developed in Japan, shiatsu is a finger-pressure technique utilizing traditional acupuncture points. Similar to acupressure, shiatsu concentrates on unblocking the flow of life energy and restoring balance in the meridians and organs in order to promote self-healing. With the client reclining, the therapist applies pressure with the finger, thumb, palm, elbow, or knee to specific zones on the skin located along the energy meridians. The treatment brings about a sense of relaxation while stimulating blood and lymphatic flow. The benefits of this treatment may include pain relief and a strengthening of the body’s resistance to disease and disorder.

Movement Therapy
A variety of techniques that utilize movement re-education and proper body mechanics in combination with massage or soft-tissue manipulation. After observing the client, the therapist will determine which corrective measures are necessary to accomplish specific goals. Active client participation is important while the practitioner uses verbal instruction, hypnosis and imagery, deep muscle and connective tissue manipulation, and mobilization in the movement re-education process. It is generally  integrated with other massage techniques.

Pregnancy Massage
Specific techniques can reduce pregnancy discomforts and concerns and enhance the physiological and emotional well-being of both mother and fetus. Skilled, appropriate touch facilitates labor, shortens labor times, and eases pain and anxiety. In the postpartum period, specialized techniques rebalance the structure, physiology, and emotions of the new mother and may help her to bond with and care for her infant. Prenatal massage therapists are trained in the anatomy, physiology, complications, precautions, and contraindications and may require referrals from physicians prior to therapy. Pregnancy Massage is available only for clients currently in their second and third trimesters. Please consult your physician if you have questions or concerns.

Thai Massage (Offered in 90 minute sessions only)
Although the origins are not completely known, credit for Thai massage is given to a famous Indian doctor, Shivago Komarpaj, who was the personal physician of the Buddha and Magadha king. Historically, manipulation was one of four major branches composing traditional Thai ceremonies or magical practices. This is based on the theory that the body is made up of seventy-two thousand sen, or energy lines, of which ten hold top priority. Thai massage also involves peripheral stimulating, meaning it acts as an external stimulant to produce specific internal effects. A significant part of the practice of Thai massage also includes yoga-like stretches which are intended to stimulate the sen. Thai massage is generally practiced on a firm mat on the floor, instrumental in the effective use of the therapist’s body weight but can also be done on a table. Except for the feet, the client remains fully clothed, so draping is not necessary. 

Foot Reflexology (Add this 25 minute treatment to your 60 minute massage.)
Based on an ancient Chinese therapy, foot reflexology involves manipulation of specific reflex areas in the foot that correspond to other parts of the body. This bodywork involves application of pressure to these reflex zones to stimulate body organs and relieve areas of congestion. It is based on the premise that energy flows through the body in meridians from the brain to the feet. Similar to acupressure principles, reflexology works with the body’s energy flow to stimulate self-healing and maintain balance in physical function. It is especially useful in stress-related illness, emotional disorders and inducing labor.

Unlike the commonly used basalt stones or lava rocks, Jade (Nephrite) stones are a complex hydrated calcium-iron-magnesium silicate. A jade stone treatment allows for a more penetrating and stimulating experience for the body because of the shapes, temperatures and energetic properties of the jade stone. The jade is shaped and polished to work effectively with the different regions of the body and are used at a variety of temperatures, hot and cold. The alternation of temperatures provides an internal workout (an aerobic experience), increasing blood flow and stimulating the lymph system and energy movement (Chi), providing for a deeper relaxed state, yet at the same time an increase in available energy. The less-heat (cold) stones flush the system, lessening toxins and stagnation, allowing the body to more naturally warm and nourish the body holistically. These internal events in turn support the immune system, and over repeated treatments will increase the vitality of the body and overall wellness.
