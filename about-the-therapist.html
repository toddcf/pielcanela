About the Therapist

Brenda Canela, CMT studied massage therapy at California Healing Arts College (CHAC) in West Los Angeles, CA. The CHAC program included Anatomy, Physiology, Hygiene and Nutrition, Aromatherapy, Swedish Massage, Chair Massage, Movement Therapy, Shiatsu, Body Mechanics, Deep Tissue, Jade (Hot/Cold) Stone Massage and Clinical Studies. After graduation, she received further training in Thai Massage and Pregnancy Massage. While she excels in deep tissue work, the nurturing factor of Prenatal Massage inspired her to continue coursework through the Institute of Somatic Therapy and earn her certification. Always looking to further her skill set, she trained with Tina Allen, founder and director of Liddle Kidz Foundation, and became internationally certified as an Infant Massage Teacher. She also received an international certification for Pediatric Massage, and further training for Touch Therapy for Childhood Cancer. She is CPR, AED and First Aid certified by The American Heart Association and is an insured member of Associated Bodywork & Massage Professionals (ABMP).

Prior to only working her private practice, Brenda worked at a spa where she was awarded the 2012 Therapist of the Year. She was also a featured Massage Therapist in the spa’s monthly newsletter. Here are some highlights:

What made you decide to get into massage?
When I was first introduced to massage, I didn't know ALL of the health benefits. I simply wanted to relax after a long day at my desk. However, when regular massage sessions relieved me of years of debilitating migraines, I was hooked. I grew interested and appreciated all of the natural benefits in the work. I wanted to be a part of that.

Why do you enjoy doing massage?
Massage is very personal. Every client is a different experience. The client trusts their therapist to cater to their needs, whether it be stress, an injury or chronic pain. It's a great feeling to know you were able to deliver. A client once sent me a message hours later that I'll never forget. It said, "I'm actually feeling joy about cooking dinner right now. A feeling I haven't had in a very long time. I missed it. I turned on music and danced a while. Thank you for the breath of life." The body heals itself, I only do what I can to facilitate that.

Massage and bodywork are also mentally stimulating for me. I, too, gain peace and balance while focused on the work I do, and that clarity allows for inspiration and enthusiasm. Win-win.

Anything else you would like our readers to know about you?
I've been an actor for about 18 years now. I fell in love with it in high school and was ridiculously fortunate enough to land my first agent then. I received my BA in Theater Arts from Loyola Marymount University and continued to work in film, television, commercials, theater and voice-over.
